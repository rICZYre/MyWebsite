Release Date:"Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood" premiered in Japan on April 5, 2009, and concluded on July 4, 2010. It ran for 64 episodes.

Both series follow the story of Edward and Alphonse Elric, two brothers who use alchemy in an attempt to bring their deceased mother back to life. However, their alchemical experiment goes wrong, resulting in Edward losing his left leg and Alphonse losing his entire body. Edward sacrifices his right arm to bind Alphonse's soul to a suit of armor. The brothers embark on a journey to find the Philosopher's Stone, a powerful alchemical artifact that they believe can restore their bodies.

While the 2003 adaptation, "Fullmetal Alchemist," diverges from the original manga's storyline, "Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood" follows the manga more faithfully. Both series are highly regarded and have garnered critical acclaim for their storytelling, characters, and themes.
