Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (Sorcerer's Stone in the U.S.)

Synopsis

Harry Potter, an orphan raised by his cruel aunt and uncle, discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard. He is invited to attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he learns about his magical heritage and the dark history surrounding the death of his parents. Harry makes close friends—Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger—and uncovers the secret of the Philosopher's Stone, a magical object that grants immortality. Harry faces off against the dark wizard Voldemort, who killed his parents, and prevents him from obtaining the Stone.



Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets

Synopsis

In his second year at Hogwarts, Harry finds himself entangled in a series of mysterious attacks on students. The school is plagued by a deadly creature released from the Chamber of Secrets, a hidden room within Hogwarts. With the help of Ron and Hermione, Harry discovers that the attacks are connected to an ancient legend about Salazar Slytherin, one of the school's founders. He ultimately confronts and defeats a young version of Voldemort, who had possessed another student, and destroys the basilisk, the creature responsible for the attacks.



Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban

Synopsis

Harry's third year at Hogwarts is overshadowed by the escape of Sirius Black, a notorious prisoner believed to have betrayed Harry's parents to Voldemort. Harry learns that Sirius is actually his godfather and was falsely accused of the crime. With the help of Hermione's Time-Turner, Harry and his friends save Sirius from execution and reveal the truth about the betrayal. The real traitor is Peter Pettigrew, who has been hiding in plain sight for years. Sirius escapes, but remains a fugitive.



Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

Synopsis

Harry is unexpectedly entered into the Triwizard Tournament, a dangerous magical competition between three wizarding schools. Despite being too young to compete, Harry is forced to participate. As the tournament progresses, Harry faces deadly challenges and uncovers a plot to resurrect Voldemort. In the final task, Harry and another competitor, Cedric Diggory, are transported to a graveyard where Voldemort is reborn. Cedric is killed, and Harry barely escapes with his life, bringing the dark news of Voldemort's return to the wizarding world.



Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix

Synopsis

In his fifth year at Hogwarts, Harry faces disbelief from the wizarding community about Voldemort's return. Meanwhile, the Ministry of Magic installs Dolores Umbridge as the new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher, who imposes strict and cruel measures on the students. Harry and his friends form Dumbledore's Army, a secret group to practice defense spells. Harry learns about a prophecy that foretells a final confrontation between him and Voldemort. The book culminates in a battle at the Ministry of Magic, where Harry's godfather, Sirius Black, is killed, and Voldemort's return is finally acknowledged by the world.



Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince

Synopsis

As Voldemort's power grows, Harry receives private lessons from Dumbledore to learn about Voldemort's past and the key to his immortality—Horcruxes, objects that contain pieces of his soul. Harry becomes suspicious of Draco Malfoy, believing he has joined the Death Eaters, Voldemort's followers. The book reveals the identity of the Half-Blood Prince as Severus Snape, who kills Dumbledore during a Death Eater attack on Hogwarts. The story ends with Harry deciding to leave school to search for and destroy the remaining Horcruxes.



Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows

Synopsis

In the final book, Harry, Ron, and Hermione embark on a dangerous quest to find and destroy Voldemort's Horcruxes. Along the way, they learn about the Deathly Hallows, three powerful magical objects that could make someone the Master of Death. The trio faces numerous challenges and battles, culminating in the Battle of Hogwarts. Voldemort is defeated when Harry sacrifices himself, but survives due to a piece of Voldemort's soul inside him being destroyed. With all Horcruxes destroyed, Harry defeats Voldemort once and for all. The epilogue shows Harry, Ron, Hermione, and their families years later, sending their own children off to Hogwarts.
