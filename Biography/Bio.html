My Little Biography
Childhood Teenage College

Childhood Years

I'm Riczyre Christian Tabigo-On, born on May 16, 2004, and I grew up in the simple neighborhood of 1246 Tuazon Barcelona Ext. in Tondo, Manila.1 My mom worked as an Overseas Filipino Worker (OFW) abroad, and my dad, a hardworking pedicab driver, supported our family from humble beginnings.2 Even though we faced money challenges, I managed to go to Isabelo de los Reyes Elementary School, where I enjoyed playing outside with friends during my early years.3

Family was a big part of shaping my values in my childhood.4 Their constant love and support set the foundation for who I am.5 emphasizing the importance of being helpful, grateful, and nice to everyone.6 My life took a big turn when my mom decided to work abroad.7

Growing up in lively Tondo, I loved the simple joys of playing outside with friends.8 Whether we were playing street basketball or just running around the neighborhood.9 Those moments created lasting memories from my childhood.10

With a strong emphasis on being helpful, grateful, and kind to all, the values I learned during my childhood became the core of who I am.11 The warmth of family gatherings, along with the smell of home-cooked meals.12 Gave me a deep sense of belonging and security, helping me face life's challenges.13

In the heart of Tondo, my family's influence and the community spirit shaped me into someone who appreciates life's simple joys.14Has a strong foundation rooted in gratitude and kindness.15 The lessons, laughter, and love from those formative years still guide me in my journey through life.16

Navigating the delicate balance of being both a mediator and a mischief-maker in our household.17 I contributed to an atmosphere filled with laughter, occasional squabbles, and a shared sense of camaraderie.18 Growing up in Tondo, I cherished the simple pleasures of playing outside with my friends, fostering a sense of joy and unity that remains an enduring part of my identity. 19

Teenage Years

During my teenage years at Tondo High School, things got tough.1 My parents decided to separate, and my mom moved abroad with her new husband, making it a challenging time for our family.2 I ended up going down a troubled path, acting out as a way to cope with the emotional turmoil.3

But things started to change during my senior high school days when the pandemic hit.4 With the support of my aunt, I found comfort in going to church every Sunday.5 This spiritual journey became a turning point, helping me become a better and stronger person.6

The pandemic made things even harder for my family, especially for my dad, who's a pedicab driver.7 Restrictions affected his work, and money became tight.8 In a surprising twist, my mom, who had been absent, reached out and wanted to help not just me but also my sister.9 She stepped up to take care of our finances, especially with my dad's health declining.10

This unexpected reunion brought stability and support to our family, something we had been missing during those tough teenage years.11 Reconnecting with my mom became a source of hope during a challenging time, showing how strong family bonds can be.12

With my mom taking care of our financial needs, it eased the pressure on my dad.13 Allowing me to focus on my education without worrying about money.15 This time of reconnection laid the foundation for healing, helping me navigate the challenges of my teenage years and emerge as a stronger and more compassionate person.16

As my mother took on the responsibility of providing for us, it not only relieved the strain on my father but also enabled me to focus on my education without the added weight of financial concerns.17

College Years

Starting college at the University of the East was a big part of my life, full of determination and a promise to make a better future.1 My mom and her husband's financial help made this academic journey possible, covering my tuition and other important expenses so I could really focus on my studies.2

Even though my mom wasn't physically there, her strong support opened up opportunities I might not have had otherwise.3 Choosing to go to the University of the East for higher education showed my deep commitment to growing personally and intellectually.4

Being at the university wasn't just about books and classes.5 I got into interesting discussions, explored different subjects, and found mentors who really pushed me.6 Outside of classes, I joined clubs and events, making connections that went beyond the usual school stuff.7

The university experience introduced me to a dynamic environment that went beyond the confines of textbooks and classrooms.8 Engaging in stimulating discussions, immersing myself in diverse academic disciplines, and finding mentors who inspired and challenged me became integral aspects of my college life.9 Beyond academics, I actively participated in various university activities, clubs, and events, fostering connections that transcended the boundaries of the classroom. 10

Living on my own during college taught me important life skills, like managing my time well and handling money wisely.11 Internships and hands-on experiences were a big part of my education, giving me insights into what I was studying and getting me ready for the working world.12 The friendships I built during this time were a strong support system, helping me through tough academic challenges and celebrating my successes as graduation approached.13

Looking back at my college years, I not only gained a lot of knowledge but also formed meaningful connections and a deep sense of achievement.12 The journey from the lively streets of Tondo to the respected halls of the University of the East played a huge role in shaping who I am.13 Giving me the confidence and purpose to take on the next phase of my life.14
